About Me

My name is Michael Allgeier. I am currently a student at Ranken Technical College pursuing an Associates of Technology Degree in Application and Web Development. I've enjoyed programming and coding ever since I discovered it and have always been the computer guy in my family. If someone had problems with any kind of technology, I was usually the go-to guy. I realized I wanted to be a developer while I was at St. Louis Community College. I didn't know what I wanted to do, and didn't have much motivation to do the school work I was assigned. During this time, I had a Mechanical Engineering friend mention that he was working on a project that involved a string of LED lights that are synced to music that is playing. This idea sounded very interesting to me, so I decided to see if I could do it. I didn't really know where to start, other than knowing that my buddy was using an arduino, so I took to google and started researching. After borrowing an arduino and some supplies from a friend, and buying other supplies that I needed, I got to work. While working on this project, I had to learn many new things such as, how circuits works, how to solder, and how to connect software with the hardware. After a couple weeks of research, building, debugging, and tinkering, I finally had a working string of LEDs that would flash to the beat of music. During this project, I discovered a strong desire for software and coding. I found it really cool to be able to toy around with how the LEDs responded, unlike the circuits, that had to be built exactly correct, I was able to mess around with the code and get the LEDs to do different things. After building this project, I realized that programming could be something I do for a career and started teaching myself HTML/CSS/Javascript before I discovered and enrolled in Ranken Technical College. Outside of computers and technology, I am a near scratch golfer and played competitively up until college. On the weekends and in my free time, I enjoy hanging out with my friends, watching Netflix, and hitting the links with some buddies or family. Recently, I have found a new love for the sport of disc golf and play multiple times per week.
